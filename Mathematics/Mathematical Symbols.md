**Set membership and containment:**

- ∈ (element of)
- ∉ (not an element of)
- ⊆ (subset or equal to)
- ⊂ (proper subset)
- ⊇ (superset or equal to)
- ⊃ (proper superset)
- ≠ (not equal)

**Set operations:**

- ∪ (union)
- ∩ (intersection)
- \ or − (set difference)
- △ or ⊕ (symmetric difference)
- × (Cartesian product)

**Special sets:**

- ∅ or {} (empty set)
- ℕ (natural numbers)
- ℤ (integers)
- ℚ (rational numbers)
- ℝ (real numbers)
- ℂ (complex numbers)
- 𝒫(A) or 2^A (power set of A)

**Set construction:**

- { } (set brackets)
- | or : (such that, in set-builder notation)

**Cardinality:**

- |A| or #A (cardinality/size of set A)
- ℵ₀ (aleph-null, cardinality of countably infinite sets)

**Logic symbols (used in set theory):**

- ⟹ or → (implies)
- ⟺ or ↔ (if and only if)
- ∀ (for all)
- ∃ (there exists)
- ∄ (there does not exist)
- ∧ (and)
- ∨ (or)
- ¬ (not)
