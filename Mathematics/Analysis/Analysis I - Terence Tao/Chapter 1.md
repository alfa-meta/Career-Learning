
We will be concerned with the questions such as the following:
1. What is a real number?
	1. Is there a largest real number?
	2. After 0, what is the "next" real number?
	3. Can you cut a real number into pieces infinitely many times?
	4. Why does a number such as 2 have a square root, while a number such as -2 does not?
	5. If there are infinitely many reals and infinitely many rationals, how come there are "more" real numbers than rational numbers.
2. How do you take the limit of a sequence of real numbers?
	1. Which sequences have limits and which ones don't?
	2. If you can stop a sequence from escaping to infinity, does this mean that is must eventually settle down and converge?
	3. Can you add infinitely many real numbers together and still get a finite real number?
	4. Can you add infinitely many rational numbers together and end up with a non-rational number?
	5. If you rearrange the elements of an infinite sum, is the sum still the same?
3. What is a function?
	1. What does it mean for a function to be continuous?
	2. Differentiable?
	3. Integrable?
	4. Bounded?
	5. Can you add infinitely many functions together?
